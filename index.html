Who Am I?

I'm a UX designer and design generalist, and I've been building my skills in the field for over three years. I earned a degree in Interactive Digital Media from U of T in 2015, and I've been working as part of a UX team at U of T ever since to design online tools and services that aim to make the lives of students and staff easier. I use design principles and ongoing feedback to inform everything I make, from sketches to prototypes to finished designs. See my UX work here!

My favourite tools to use for my work are Sketch, Balsamiq, InVision, Axure, Photoshop CS6, Illustrator CS6, HTML and CSS.

    I have lots of experience doing the following:

  • Usability tests, interviews and card sorts
  • Heuristic and accessibility analyses (focusing on WCAG 2.0, AA level).
  • Identifying user needs via personas, user story maps, and user stories.
  • Low and high-fidelity wireframing and prototyping using all the above as a guide.

I'm also a hobbyist artist and I post some of my favourite pieces on the art section of this site.

I'm currently learning Unity to make my first solo game, which will be playable here soon. Read my blog if you're interested in the journey so far in applying UX practices to game design.
